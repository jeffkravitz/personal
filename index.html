Welcome!

I am a psychology researcher looking for a new research position, where I aim to further develop my skillset and experience as I prepare for doctoral applications. In my most recent position, I served as the lab manager for the Curtis Lab at New York University, where we research spatial cognition and working memory using computational neuroscience. I earned my M.A. in Experimental Psychology from Brooklyn College and my B.S. in Psychology from Stony Brook University. My research has focused on a) how social-evolutionary perspectives can explain the relationship between moral judgment and emotion, b) whether perceptions of self stress can cause harsher moral judgment, and c) if mindfulness meditation can reduce inherent bias in decision making.

My goals include completing a doctorate in psychology, and my long term goal is to become a professor of psychology at a research university.

About Me

I am a researcher who has primarily worked in the fields of social psychology and neuroscience. I earned my M.A. in Experimental Psychology from Brooklyn College, and my B.S in Psychology from Stony Brook University. While completing my Masters, I worked in the Emotion and Morality Lab with Dr. Hanah Chapman. My thesis in this program focused on the different emotions people feel and their effect on moral judgment. While in the lab, I designed and conducted a variety of experiments and taught several undergraduate courses including statistics and psychology methods. After finishing the program, I worked as the Lab Manager for the Curtis Lab at New York University's Department of Psychology and Center for Neural Science. My work there included programming experiment software for use with MRI and TMS methodology, and analysis of various kinds of psychophysiological data. Currently, I am seeking a new position as a researcher.

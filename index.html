About Me

My career has primarily been in the fields of psychology and neuroscience. I earned my M.A. in Experimental Psychology from Brooklyn College, and my B.S in Psychology from Stony Brook University. While completing my Masters, I learned how to program in R to create reproducible analysis scripts. After finishing the program, I worked as the Lab Manager for the Curtis Lab at New York University's Department of Psychology and Center for Neural Science. My work there included programming experiment software for use with MRI and TMS methodology, and analysis of various kinds of psychophysiological data. During my experience with research, I discovered my love of programming. I enjoy the process of creative problem solving to produce solutions to tough problems. My goal is to enroll in a Masters in Computer Science in order to follow my passion of developing software.
